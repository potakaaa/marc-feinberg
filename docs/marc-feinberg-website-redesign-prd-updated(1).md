# Marc Feinberg Website Redesign PRD Prompt

## Short Prompt

Rebuild MarcFeinberg.com into a modern, responsive, conversion-focused personal brand and coaching website for Marc Feinberg, a Christian high-performance coach, business/life coach, author, speaker, and mastermind facilitator. The main goal is conversions: new eyeballs, new conversations, new booked calls, and new potential business.

Use the actual images and brand assets from the current website where available, including Marc’s signature logo, portrait photos, coaching/client photos, testimonial video thumbnails, TherapyTribe badge, and book cover images. Keep the real credibility assets, but redesign the layout around them so the site feels premium, clean, modern, and easier to trust. Do not replace Marc’s real photos with generic stock images unless an asset is missing.

Design the site with a simple, sleek, modern UI using clean sans-serif typography only. Avoid serif fonts because they make the design feel more traditional and outdated. The site should feel high-end, warm, faith-driven, performance-focused, and trustworthy without being overly flashy. Improve navigation, spacing, hierarchy, CTA placement, mobile responsiveness, and readability.

You may restructure the current website completely if it improves conversions. Recommended routes: Home, Services, Books, Blog, About, and Contact/Schedule Call. Use a static website approach with no backend required. Use external links for Calendly scheduling, email, phone, book purchases, social links, and embedded testimonial videos. Prioritize fast loading, clear messaging, strong social proof, and repeated “Schedule Free Call” calls-to-action.

## Long PRD Prompt

Act as a senior product designer, conversion copywriter, UX strategist, and frontend website builder. Rebuild MarcFeinberg.com into a modern, responsive, premium personal brand website for Marc Feinberg. The goal is not only to improve the visuals, but to increase conversions: new eyeballs, new conversations, new booked calls, and new potential coaching, speaking, and mastermind clients.

Marc is a Christian life and business coach, high-performance coach, author, speaker, mastermind facilitator, certified biblical counselor, and advisor. His audience includes professional athletes, entrepreneurs, executives, business leaders, faith-driven leaders, and high achievers who want to break through performance ceilings, remove self-limiting beliefs, strengthen their purpose, and execute at a higher level.

## Primary Website Goal

The website must be designed around lead generation and trust-building.

Primary conversions:
1. Schedule a free call.
2. Start a conversation with Marc.
3. Build trust quickly with qualified visitors.
4. Present Marc as a credible, faith-driven, high-performance coach.
5. Make services, books, speaking, and mastermind offers easy to understand.
6. Encourage visitors to take action without feeling pressured.

Marc’s feedback is important: “I need conversions. I need new clients. Anything that we do with the website needs to bring in new eyeballs, new conversations, and new potential business.”

## Brand Direction

The design should feel:
- Simple
- Sleek
- Modern
- Premium
- Trustworthy
- Wise
- Faith-driven
- High-performance
- Warm but professional
- Confident but not arrogant
- Inspirational but clear

Avoid making the site look too corporate, generic, outdated, cluttered, or overly flashy. It should feel personal, direct, and credible, like a trusted advisor for champions, entrepreneurs, and leaders.

## Typography Requirement

Use clean modern sans-serif typography only. Do not use serif fonts because they can make the site feel dated or overly traditional.

Preferred font direction:
- Headings: strong modern sans-serif, bold, confident, easy to read
- Body: clean sans-serif with excellent readability
- Good options: Inter, Manrope, Sora, Plus Jakarta Sans, DM Sans, or similar
- Avoid decorative, script, or serif fonts for page content

Marc’s signature logo can remain as an image/brand asset, but the website’s actual text system should use sans-serif fonts.

## Image and Asset Requirements

Use the actual images and brand assets from the current website wherever available. Do not replace Marc’s real photos with generic stock photos unless an asset is missing.

Use and redesign around these existing assets:
- Marc Feinberg signature logo in the header
- Hero portrait of Marc in a suit standing near the car
- Coaching/client credibility photos from the “Why Work With Marc Feinberg” section
- Photos with notable figures and clients

The current site already has strong credibility assets. The redesign should preserve those real assets, but improve how they are displayed.

Image treatment requirements:

- Use the portrait image strategically in the hero and About sections, but avoid repeating it too much in a way that feels redundant
